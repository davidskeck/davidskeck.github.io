"Anything You Want" - Book review

Jul 7, 2014

I had the chance to read this book about a week ago. I expected to just read the first few pages before heading into work, but I found it so enthralling that I read the whole thing that morning. It only took about an hour, just like Derek said it would in the first chapter.

As you can tell, this book is pretty short. The content feels almost obsessively pared down to the most relevant message possible.

I love this book is because its voice is so lucid. Everything was so clear and easy to follow.

The chapters of the book are just dripping with understanding. Each scenario offers a chance to see a new perspective and step back from what might be considered “common sense” to illuminate an important insight that was made.

At the end of the book, Sivers invites anyone who has “made it this far” to email him with any comments or questions. I took advantage of this offer and I received an email back the very next day. It is obvious he cares about his readers, and it was nice to know that Sivers embodies the qualities he discusses in this book.

I feel like I learned a lot, and I definitely laughed a lot, but the most important thing I experienced from reading this book was an overwhelming sense of optimism and empowerment.

Rating: 10/10
